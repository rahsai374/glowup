GlowUp — Branding Placement

3 approaches for how the app name appears (or doesn't) across authenticated screens.

★ Recommended — Option A

Implicit branding. "GlowUp" appears on Splash only. Every authenticated screen uses the design language (terracotta, Fraunces, blobs) as brand recall. The tab bar + consistent palette = brand presence without repeating the wordmark. Premium, confident — how top consumer apps (Airbnb, Calm, Headspace) behave post-login.

    Pros

  • ✓ Brand name present but not shoved in; feels like product copy
  • ✓ "GlowUp" used as a verb/journey strengthens brand positioning

    • Cons

  • ✗ Harder to maintain consistently across i18n (Hindi translations)
  • ✗ Some screens feel forced ("Your GlowUp Profile" is a stretch)

    • Recommendation: Option A

    The GlowUp brand is already expressed on every screen through its design language — the terracotta, Fraunces headings, and ambient blobs are unmistakably GlowUp. Adding the wordmark to authenticated screens doesn't increase brand recognition; it adds noise. The Splash screen plants the name. The Share Card reinforces it (with a "GlowUp" badge). That's the right cadence — brand-forward at entry, content-forward throughout.

    One small enhancement worth considering: add a subtle ✦ GlowUp badge to the Share Card (already planned in Screen 14) and possibly to the Home greeting subline only — "Your skin, your GlowUp" — without touching any other screens.
