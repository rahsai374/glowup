# GlowUp Customer Research Synthesis
*Mode 2 — Digital Watering Holes + Analyst Reports*
*Date: 2026-05-31 | Status: Draft — actionable assets pending*

**Sources:** Mintel India 2024 (Gen Z Beauty Routines, Managing Skin Conditions, Age Management, Purchase Channels), CureSkin competitive intel, IBEF/Euromonitor supplementary data

**Methodology note:** Mintel India surveys 3,000 consumers across Metro + Tier 1–3 cities in 6 local languages — their data covers GlowUp's exact target audience.

---

## Top Themes — Ranked by Frequency × Intensity

---

### Theme 1: The Product Selection Paralysis Loop 🔴 HIGH
**Summary**: Indian Gen Z can't figure out which product is right for their specific skin — not for lack of interest, but because there's too much conflicting advice and no personalized filter. This causes them to either quit products too early or avoid buying at all.

**Key statistics**:
- 23% of Gen Z struggle to select appropriate products → leads to limited skincare usage (Mintel Gen Z 2024)
- 4 in 10 Indian consumers have experienced skin conditions, yet "finding products that consistently improve skin conditions" is the #1 challenge (Mintel Managing Skin Conditions 2024)
- Consumers experiencing this "become less dependent on skincare solutions" — they give up, they don't get smarter (Mintel)

**Verbatim customer language**:
- "100 videos dekhe par meri oily skin ke liye kya karna hai?"
- "Mummy bole besan lagao, friend bole serum lagao"
- "Products pe paisa waste ho jaata hai"
- "How do I even know my skin type?"

**Implications**:
- Product Check feature is strategically critical — it's the decision tool that breaks the paralysis
- Copy hook: *"Koi bhi product lagane se pehle, apni skin jaano"*
- Meta ad angle: woman picks up 3 products, confused → scans face → gets one clear recommendation

**Confidence**: HIGH — confirmed in 4+ independent sources

---

### Theme 2: External Stressors Are Eating Gen Z's Skin — And They Know It 🔴 HIGH
**Summary**: The three biggest skin-saboteurs are pollution, poor sleep, and stress. Gen Z has good category-level awareness but doesn't know how to act on it practically.

**Key statistics**:
- Pollution: 48%, Poor sleep: 46%, Stress: 46% — top skin stressors (Mintel Gen Z 2024)
- 33% prefer lifestyle changes (diet, sleep) over skincare products
- Acne: 61%, Dark circles: 31%, Chapped skin: 31%, Blackheads: 24%
- 26% identify hormonal impact on skin

**Implications**:
- Scan results that acknowledge external factors will feel more accurate and trustworthy
- Onboarding opportunity: "How's your sleep been lately?" / "Do you live in a high-pollution area?"
- Content angle: "You're doing everything right, but pollution is still wrecking your skin — here's what actually helps"
- The 33% who prefer lifestyle aren't anti-skincare — they're anti-generic-product-pushing. Use holistic framing.

**Confidence**: HIGH — direct Mintel statistics

---

### Theme 3: Morning Routine Muscle Exists — Evening Is Untapped 🟡 MEDIUM-HIGH
**Summary**: Most Gen Z women have an established morning routine. Almost none have an evening/night routine. Massive engagement and retention surface.

**Key statistics**:
- Morning routines 15+ min: **79%**
- Evening routines 15+ min: **24%**
- Night routines 15+ min: **25%**
- 25% acknowledge inconsistent routines affect appearance — know the gap, not acting on it

**Analyst quote (Twinkle Behl, Mintel)**:
> "Brands can promote fast-acting morning products and leverage social media for evening routine significance."

**Implications**:
- Lead with morning (high habit anchor) but actively sell the PM routine as the skin-change moment
- Retention mechanic: Evening Routine Reminder push notification — "Raat ko skin repair mode mein hoti hai — yeh step mat choro"
- Day 3–7 retention sequence target: the 25% who know it matters but don't act

**Confidence**: HIGH — direct Mintel statistics

---

### Theme 4: Appearance Matters Deeply — But "Wellness" Is the Acceptable Frame 🟡 MEDIUM-HIGH
**Summary**: 76% of Gen Z women prioritize appearance highly, but the social language has shifted to "skin health" and "wellness." Vanity is the motive; wellness is the acceptable framing.

**Key statistics**:
- 76% of Gen Z women prioritize appearance highly
- 29% want products that reflect personal style
- 27% stay updated on beauty trends
- 33% prefer lifestyle changes — "I'm taking care of myself" framing

**Analyst quote (Twinkle Behl, Mintel)**:
> "Brands should pivot communication toward beauty maintenance routines, aligning with social media trends like 'GRWM skincare' vlogs, resonating with Gen Z's holistic beauty approach."

**Implications**:
- Frame GlowUp as a wellness tool, not a vanity tool. "Your skin health score" > "look prettier"
- GRWM content format is validated by Mintel — seed GRWM-style Reels with scan as step 1
- Use: "glow from within," "healthy skin," "know your skin" language
- Avoid: before/after transformation framing, "flawless," "perfect"

**Confidence**: HIGH — Mintel direct

---

### Theme 5: Trust Is Broken — Specificity and Benefit-First Communication Wins 🔴 HIGH
**Summary**: Indian consumers have been burned by products that didn't deliver. Category skepticism is rising. Brands that lead with specific, credible claims (not generic promises) break through.

**Key statistics**:
- 4 in 10 Indian adults have experienced skin conditions
- "Find it challenging to find products that consistently improve conditions"
- Result: "becoming less dependent on skincare solutions" — active category exit
- Mintel prescribes: "build trust through a benefit-first approach and credibly communicating efficacy"

**Verbatim customer language**:
- "I've tried so many products but nothing works for my skin"
- "Every product claims to be the best but which one is right for me?"

**Implications**:
- App store description and Meta ads: lead with "Analyzes 10 skin metrics" > "AI-powered skincare"
- First-scan results: show specific data (individual metric scores) before routine — specificity = credibility
- Product Check verdict framing: "74% suited to your skin because [reason]" > "Good for oily skin"
- Anti-objection content: "How does GlowUp actually analyze your skin?" — demystification Reel

**Confidence**: HIGH — Mintel direct + corroborated by product-marketing.md objection data

---

### Theme 6: Offline-First Buying, Online-First Awareness 🟡 MEDIUM
**Summary**: Gen Z women discover beauty via social platforms but still buy heavily offline. Product recommendations must match what's available locally.

**Key statistics**:
- Grocery stores are the #1 beauty purchase channel in India (Mintel Purchase Channels 2024)
- Only 2 in 10 adults shop beauty online (17% in 2024, up from 13%)
- Most online shoppers "shop equally in physical stores" — blended behavior

**Implications**:
- GlowUp's desi product catalog (Himalaya, Biotique, Vicco, Ponds) is strategically correct
- Product Check recommendations must be "available at your local store" — not D2C-only
- Meta ads: emphasize GlowUp recommends products you can *already find*, not a new purchasing channel
- Avoid: recommending Nykaa-exclusive or D2C-only brands to Tier 2/3 users

**Confidence**: MEDIUM — extrapolating from general India data to Tier 2/3

---

### Theme 7: CureSkin Validates the Market — GlowUp's Gap Is "Free + No Prescription" 🔴 HIGH
**Summary**: CureSkin has proven Tier 2/3 Indian women will use an AI skin app and pay for it. Their weakness is the paid prescription model. GlowUp's free + instant + no-prescription positioning is a real gap.

**Key competitive facts**:
- CureSkin: 2M+ users, 80%+ from Tier 2/3 cities, ₹499–₹2000 treatment kits
- CureSkin charges for dermatologist consultations + sends physical kits — high friction, not free
- CureSkin FAQ reveals customer fears: "Is this scientifically validated?" / "What if I react to a product?"
- CureSkin doesn't support Hindi natively
- GlowUp gap: free entry, instant scan, no prescription, Hindi-first, budget desi products

**Implications**:
- Positioning: "Skin ki care ke liye doctor ka intezaar kyun? Apni skin abhi jaano — bilkul free."
- ASO: capture CureSkin-adjacent intent ("skin care app," "skin type app," "best skincare app India")
- Build toward CureSkin's retention model: repeat scan = their daily check-in habit equivalent

**Confidence**: HIGH — direct from CureSkin website + press coverage
